Welcome to SolarVoyage: Your Portal to the Cosmos

Welcome to SolarVoyage, your gateway to the wonders of our solar system and beyond! Whether you're an aspiring astronaut, a space enthusiast, or simply curious about the mysteries of the universe, you've come to the right place.

At SolarVoyage, we're dedicated to making the vast expanse of our solar system accessible to everyone. Our mission is to provide you with the tools and knowledge needed to plan your own celestial adventure. From the fiery surface of the Sun to the distant reaches of Pluto, our interactive platform will help you explore, learn, and dream big.

What we can offer to you:

  • Understand the characteristics and environments of each planet.
  • Know the cost and optimal time for visiting each planet.
  • Provide safety measures.

Before the journey to space, we'd like to remind you of the following several points:

  • Please confirm your physical health first. If you have serious health problems, such as heart disease, respiratory disease, and severe mental illness, we don’t recommend you start a space trip.
  • Your body must be able to withstand weightlessness.
  • We will try our best to plan a complete journey, but once in outer space, everything becomes variable, and we won’t be able to ensure what problems we will encounter in outer space.

Mercury Visit

  • -173°C to 427°C/-279.4 to 800.6°F
  • 5% the mass of Earth
  • 38% the size of Earth (The smallest planet)
  • 7 times brighter than Earth
  • No moon
  • Type: Rocky planet
  • The distance to Earth: 77-222 million km
  • Atmosphere: Instead of an atmosphere, Mercury possesses a thin exosphere composed of O2, Na, H2, He, and K.

Venus Visit

  • Approximately 460°C/860°F (The hottest planet)
  • 82% the mass of Earth
  • 95% the size of Earth
  • No moon
  • Type: Rocky planet
  • The distance to Earth: 38-261 million km
  • Only rotates clockwise (called retrograde rotation).
  • Atmosphere: Mostly CO2, with clouds composed of H2S04.
  • Strong greenhouse effect.

Earth

  • -89.2 to 56.7°C/-128.56 to 134.06°F
  • Mass: 5.97*1024kg
  • Size: 12,742 km radius
  • 1 moon: the Moon
  • Type: Rocky planet
  • Atmosphere: Consists of 78% N2, 21% O2, and 1% other gases.

Mars Visit

  • -140 to 30°C/-220 to 86°F
  • 10% the mass of Earth
  • 50% the size of Earth
  • 2 moons: Phobos & Deimos
  • Type: Rocky planet
  • The distance to Earth: 55.7-401 million km
  • The cycle of seasons is similar to Earth's.
  • Atmosphere: CO2, N2, Ar.

Jupiter Visit

  • -161 to -108°C/-257.8 to -162.4 °F
  • 318 times the mass of Earth
  • 11 times the size of Earth
  • 79 moons
  • Type: Gas giant
  • The distance to Earth: 588-928 milloin km
  • No solid surface.
  • Atmosphere:
    • The top cloud: ammonia ice
    • The middle layer: [NH4]SH
    • The innermost layer: water, ice, and vapor

Saturn Visit

  • -189 to -139°C/-308.2 to -218.2°F
  • 95 times the mass of Earth
  • 9 times the size of Earth
  • 146 moons
  • Type: Gas giant
  • The distance to Earth: 1.2-1.7 billion km
  • No solid surface.
  • Atmosphere: Blanketed with clouds that appear as faint stripes, jet streams, and storms.

Uranus Visit

  • -226 to -197°C/-374.8 to -322.6°F
  • 15 times the mass of Earth
  • 4 times the size of Earth
  • Type: Ice giant
  • The distance to Earth: 2.58-3.15 billion km
  • Inner rings are darker; outer rings are brighter colored.
  • No solid surface.
  • Atmosphere: Mostly of H2 and He2 with just a little bit of CH4 and NH3.

Neptune Visit

  • -218 to -200 °C
  • 4 times the mass of Earth
  • 17 times the size of Earth
  • Type: Ice giant
  • The distance to Earth: 4.32 - 4.17 billion km
  • The only planet in the solar system not visible to the naked eye.
  • Atmosphere: Mostly of H2 and He2 with just a little bit of CH4.
  • Neptune's blue color is caused by the CH₄ found in its atmosphere.

(No to scale)
