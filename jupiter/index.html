  Launch History

    • First Launch
       。Pioneer 10：Pioneer 10 made the first flyby of Jupiter in December 1973, marking humanity's first mission to explore Jupiter. It was followed by Pioneer 11 a few months later. Besides capturing the first close-up images of Jupiter, the spacecraft discovered Jupiter's magnetic field and probed its major fluid interiors.

        Ⅰ. Great Red Spot

        Located in the southern hemisphere of Jupiter, the Great Red Spot is a massive storm with a width approximately twice that of Earth. It consists of counterclockwise-rotating deep-red clouds and has wind speeds exceeding any storm on Earth.
        It was first observed in 1878, and earlier mentions of what is believed to be the Great Red Spot date back to 1665 when Gian Domenico Cassini referred to it as a "permanent storm." The long-lasting nature of the storm can be explained by the fact that Jupiter lacks a solid surface. On Earth, hurricanes dissipate when they reach solid ground, but the Great Red Spot has nothing solid beneath it.
