Mercury

  Traveling To Mercury！

Mercury is the fastest-moving planet among those orbiting the Sun, which is why it is named Mercury,who was the god who delivered messages for the gods.

Mysterious Mercury appears desolate and barren, but deep inside, there are clues pointing to a different past.

This stop is one of the hottest region in the solar system, if you want to experience a space journey that takes you as close as 0.48 astronomical units (AU) from the Sun, this would be an excellent choice！

        Attractions

        Caloris Basin

        Ⅰ. Caloris Basin

        It is a massive impact crater on Mercury, with a diameter of approximately 1550 kilometers. It is one of the largest impact craters in the solar system. This crater was discovered in 1974 and is surrounded by a circular mountain range that rises to about 2 kilometers in height.


        Ⅱ. Caloris Montes

        It is a mountain peak located on the edge of the Caloris Basin on Mercury. Its highest peak reaches approximately 3,000 meters, making it the tallest point on Mercury. The central coordinates are located at 39.4°N 187.2°W.
